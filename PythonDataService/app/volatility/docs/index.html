Overview

This module converts raw option chain data (strike, time-to-maturity, market price) into a queryable implied volatility surface. It supports three fitting methods:

MethodTypeBest forSpeed
Variance interpolationNon-parametric Dense, liquid chainsFast
SABRParametric (4 params) Rates, FX, moderate skewFast
SVIParametric (5 params) Equities, strong skewMedium

1. Implied Volatility Solver

The IV solver finds the volatility $\sigma$ such that the Black-Scholes-Merton model price matches the observed market price.

Black-Scholes-Merton Model

For a European call option:

$$C = S_0 \, e^{-qT} \, \Phi(d_1) - K \, e^{-rT} \, \Phi(d_2)$$

where

$$d_1 = \frac{\ln(S_0/K) + (r - q + \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2)T}{\sigma\sqrt{T}}, \qquad d_2 = d_1 - \sigma\sqrt{T}$$

and $\Phi(\cdot)$ is the standard normal CDF.

Solver Strategy

Primary: QuantLib's VanillaOption.impliedVolatility() using the AnalyticEuropeanEngine with a BlackScholesMertonProcess.

Fallback: Scipy's brentq root-finder on the BS pricing function, bracketed between $\sigma \in [0.005, 5.0]$.

2. Variance Interpolation

The simplest method: linear interpolation on total implied variance $w(K) = \sigma^2(K) \cdot T$ as a function of strike. This ensures that calendar-spread no-arbitrage is preserved when interpolating between expiries.

$$\sigma_{\text{interp}}(K, T) = \sqrt{\frac{w(K, T)}{T}}$$

where $w$ is linearly interpolated in both the strike and time dimensions.

Pros: Exact recovery at input strikes, no parametric assumptions.
Cons: Extrapolation outside the strike range is flat (no smile wings). Requires dense data.

3. SABR Model

The SABR (Stochastic Alpha Beta Rho) model describes the forward price and its volatility as correlated diffusions:

$$dF_t = \alpha_t \, F_t^\beta \, dW_1, \qquad d\alpha_t = \nu \, \alpha_t \, dW_2$$ $$dW_1 \cdot dW_2 = \rho \, dt$$

Hagan Approximation (2002)

The implied volatility approximation for $K \neq F$:

$$\sigma_B(K, F) = \frac{\alpha}{(FK)^{(1-\beta)/2} \left[1 + \frac{(1-\beta)^2}{24}\ln^2\frac{F}{K} + \frac{(1-\beta)^4}{1920}\ln^4\frac{F}{K}\right]} \cdot \frac{z}{x(z)} \cdot \left[1 + \left(\frac{(1-\beta)^2}{24}\frac{\alpha^2}{(FK)^{1-\beta}} + \frac{\rho\beta\nu\alpha}{4(FK)^{(1-\beta)/2}} + \frac{2-3\rho^2}{24}\nu^2\right)T\right]$$

where $z = \frac{\nu}{\alpha}(FK)^{(1-\beta)/2}\ln\frac{F}{K}$ and $x(z) = \ln\frac{\sqrt{1-2\rho z + z^2}+z-\rho}{1-\rho}$.

Parameters

ParameterSymbolRangeMeaning
alpha$\alpha$$> 0$ATM vol level
beta$\beta$$[0, 1]$CEV exponent (fixed; 0.5 for rates, 1.0 for equities)
rho$\rho$$(-1, 1)$Correlation (controls skew)
nu$\nu$$> 0$Vol-of-vol (controls smile curvature)

Recommended default: $\beta = 0.5$ for equities. Fix $\beta$ and calibrate $(\alpha, \rho, \nu)$ via least-squares.

4. SVI Parameterisation

Gatheral's Stochastic Volatility Inspired (SVI) model parameterises total implied variance $w(k) = \sigma^2(k) \cdot T$ as a function of log-moneyness $k = \ln(K/F)$:

$$w(k) = a + b\left[\rho(k - m) + \sqrt{(k-m)^2 + \sigma^2}\right]$$

Parameters

ParameterRangeMeaning
$a$$\mathbb{R}$Overall variance level
$b$$\geq 0$Slope of the wings
$\rho$$(-1, 1)$Skew (rotation of the smile)
$m$$\mathbb{R}$Horizontal translation
$\sigma$$> 0$Smoothness at the vertex

No-Arbitrage Constraints

Butterfly condition:

$$a + b\sigma\sqrt{1-\rho^2} \geq 0$$

This ensures $w(k) \geq 0$ for all $k$, preventing negative implied variance.

Pros: Excellent fit for equity smiles; 5 parameters capture skew and kurtosis.
Cons: Not a stochastic model (just a parameterisation); needs careful initialisation.

5. Surface Builder

The VolSurfaceBuilder assembles a full surface in three stages:

Stage 1: IV Solving

Each option record $(K_i, T_j, V_{\text{mkt}})$ is passed through the solver to obtain $\sigma_{\text{imp}}(K_i, T_j)$.

Stage 2: Per-Expiry Fitting

Records are grouped by expiry $T_j$. Each group forms a smile slice that is fitted with the chosen method. Slices with fewer than min_contracts_per_slice points are discarded.

Stage 3: Cross-Expiry Interpolation

For queries at arbitrary $(K, T)$, the surface interpolates between the two nearest fitted slices using variance-time weighting:

$$w(K, T) = (1-\lambda)\,w(K, T_{\text{lo}}) + \lambda\,w(K, T_{\text{hi}})$$ $$\lambda = \frac{T - T_{\text{lo}}}{T_{\text{hi}} - T_{\text{lo}}}$$ $$\sigma(K, T) = \sqrt{\frac{w(K,T)}{T}}$$

This preserves the calendar-spread no-arbitrage condition $w(K, T_1) \leq w(K, T_2)$ for $T_1 \leq T_2$, provided individual slices are monotone in total variance.

6. API Endpoints

POST /api/volatility/build

Build an IV surface from option chain data.

{
  "ticker": "AAPL",
  "spot": 185.50,
  "rate": 0.053,
  "method": "svi",
  "options": [
    {"strike": 180, "ttm": 0.0822, "option_price": 7.20, "is_call": true},
    {"strike": 185, "ttm": 0.0822, "option_price": 4.50, "is_call": true},
    ...
  ]
}

Returns: fitted parameters, diagnostics, arbitrage checks, and a surface_id for subsequent queries.

POST /api/volatility/query/{surface_id}

Query the surface at specific (strike, TTM) points.

{
  "queries": [
    {"strike": 185, "ttm": 0.25},
    {"strike": 190, "ttm": 0.5}
  ]
}

POST /api/volatility/grid/{surface_id}

Evaluate the surface on a regular strike × TTM grid for plotting.

7. Arbitrage Checks

After fitting, each smile slice is tested for:

Butterfly Arbitrage

The second derivative of total variance with respect to log-moneyness must be non-negative (convexity of the call price surface):

$$\frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial k^2} \geq 0$$

Violations indicate the smile has a concave region where a butterfly spread would have negative cost.

Calendar Spread Arbitrage

Total variance must be non-decreasing in time:

$$w(k, T_1) \leq w(k, T_2) \quad \text{for } T_1 \leq T_2$$

The variance-time interpolation method ensures this by construction.

9. Common Failure Cases

Bad Data

Stale quotes, wide bid-ask spreads, and illiquid deep-OTM options produce IV solver failures. Filter records by open_interest > 10 and bid-ask spread < 20% before building.

Sparse Strikes

With fewer than 5 strikes per expiry, parametric fits (SABR, SVI) are under-determined. Use variance interpolation for sparse data, or relax min_contracts_per_slice.

Near-Expiry Options

Options with $T < 1$ day are rejected by the solver. Options with $T < 7$ days have high gamma and may produce unstable IVs.

Deep OTM / ITM

Deep OTM options have near-zero prices, making IV recovery ill-conditioned. Deep ITM options may violate intrinsic value bounds in illiquid markets. Both are flagged with appropriate status codes.
