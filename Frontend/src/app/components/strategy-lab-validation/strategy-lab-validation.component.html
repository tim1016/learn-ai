Documentation

Strategy Rules ENTRY / EXIT

Entry Conditions (ALL must be true simultaneously)

  1. EMA5 crosses ABOVE EMA10 on the current 15-min candle CLOSE (fresh crossover only)
  2. EMA Gap = (EMA5 - EMA10) must be ≥ 0.20 at the crossover candle
  3. RSI (14-period) must be between 50 and 70 at the crossover candle
  4. Enter at the CLOSE of the crossover candle

Exit Condition

  • Exit exactly 5 candles (75 minutes) after entry at CLOSE
  • If the 5th candle is after market close, exit at next open

Skip Conditions (DO NOT TRADE)

  • RSI < 50 or RSI > 70 at signal
  • EMA gap < 0.20 (crossover too weak)
  • Not a FRESH crossover (EMA5 was already above EMA10 on the previous bar)
Validation Methodology MATCHING

How Trades Are Matched

The validation engine uses greedy nearest-neighbor matching by entry timestamp:

  1. For each reference trade, find the closest reproduced trade whose entry timestamp is within 15 minutes.
  2. Once matched, a reproduced trade cannot be reused for another reference trade.
  3. Unmatched reference trades are labeled "Ref Only" (signal found in Excel but not reproduced).
  4. Unmatched reproduced trades are labeled "Ours Only" (signal found by engine but not in Excel).

What "Match" Means

A trade is considered matched when the engine finds a corresponding entry within the 15-minute tolerance window. The PnL and prices may still differ due to data source differences, rounding, or resampling artifacts.

Common Discrepancy Sources

  • Resampling: The CSV has 1-min bars; we resample to 15-min. Anchor alignment (9:30 ET) may produce slightly different bar boundaries.
  • Indicator warm-up: EMA and RSI values at the start of the dataset may differ if the Excel used a longer history.
  • Market close handling: The engine uses fixed 5-bar exit without special overnight logic.
Metrics Definitions REFERENCE
Win Rate

Percentage of trades with positive PnL. = Winning Trades / Total Trades

Profit Factor

Ratio of gross profit to gross loss. = Sum(winning PnL%) / |Sum(losing PnL%)|. Values > 1.0 indicate profitability.

Expectancy

Average PnL% per trade. = Total PnL% / Total Trades. Positive values mean the strategy has a statistical edge.

Max Drawdown

Largest peak-to-trough decline in cumulative PnL%. Measures worst-case scenario for the strategy.

Sharpe Ratio

Risk-adjusted return. = (Mean PnL% / Std PnL%) * sqrt(252). Higher is better; > 1.0 is generally acceptable.

Win/Loss Ratio

Average winning trade size divided by average losing trade size. = |Avg Win%| / |Avg Loss%|.
Data Pipeline TECHNICAL

Processing Steps

  1. Parse CSV: Read 1-minute OHLCV bars from the uploaded file. Supports unix_ts or iso_time timestamp formats.
  2. RTH Filter: Keep only Regular Trading Hours bars (9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET) using the NYSE calendar.
  3. Resample to 15-min: Aggregate 1-min bars into 15-min bars anchored to 9:30 ET. OHLCV aggregation: open=first, high=max, low=min, close=last, volume=sum.
  4. Compute Indicators: Calculate EMA(5), EMA(10), RSI(14), ADX(14) using pandas-ta on the 15-min close prices. Pre-computed indicators from the CSV are NOT used (they were computed on 1-min data).
  5. Run Strategy: Detect fresh EMA crossovers, apply gap + RSI filters, generate trades with 5-bar fixed exit.

Why Recompute Indicators?

The uploaded CSV contains indicators computed on 1-minute closes. After resampling to 15-minute bars, those values are invalid. EMA(5) on 1-min data covers 5 minutes; EMA(5) on 15-min data covers 75 minutes. The strategy requires 15-min indicators.
Known Limitations CAVEATS
  • Market close exit: The engine uses a strict 5-bar exit. The Excel strategy exits at next open if the 5th bar falls after market close. This may cause PnL differences for trades entered late in the session.
  • Indicator warm-up: The first ~200 bars of EMA/RSI may differ from the Excel if it used a longer history for warm-up.
  • Data source: Minor price differences between the CSV source (Polygon) and TradingView/broker data can produce different crossover signals.
  • ADX filter: The Excel strategy does not use an ADX minimum filter. The engine defaults to no ADX filter (adx_min=None), matching the Excel behavior.
