Momentum RSI + Stochastic Strategy

State-based intraday bullish momentum alignment

Strategy Overview

This strategy identifies short-term bullish momentum alignment using RSI, a dual moving average trend filter, and the Stochastic Oscillator. It is designed as an intraday-only strategy with forced end-of-day exit to avoid overnight risk.

Core Concept

RSI Regime Filter Only enter when RSI is in a neutral band (40–60). Avoid overbought/oversold extremes.
MA Trend Confirmation Fast MA > Slow MA confirms bullish trend. Price above Fast MA confirms the asset is not in a deep pullback.
Stochastic Momentum %K > %D indicates short-term momentum is accelerating upward.
EOD Exit All positions closed 15 minutes before market close. No overnight holding.
State-Based vs. Crossover-Based This strategy uses state conditions (e.g., %K > %D) rather than crossover events (e.g., %K crosses above %D). This dramatically increases the number of trade opportunities while maintaining the same directional bias. A crossover requirement on the same bar is extremely restrictive and produces very few trades.
Long Only

Only long positions are taken. The strategy does not short.

No Overnight

All positions exit before market close to eliminate overnight gap risk.

Max 1 Position

Only one position open at a time. No pyramiding or scaling in.

Next-Bar Entry

Signal fires on bar t; entry occurs at open of bar t+1 to avoid look-ahead bias.

Entry Execution When all conditions are met on bar , the position is entered at the open price of bar . This is a critical no-look-ahead safeguard — you cannot trade at the close of a bar you haven't yet observed.

Exit Conditions

All open positions are closed at a fixed time before the market close.

Time-Based Exit

For US equities, the market closes at 16:00 ET. With the default 15-minute exit parameter, all positions are closed at 15:45. If using larger timeframes (e.g., 15-minute bars), the exit occurs at the closest bar on or after the exit time.

Position closed at end of period If the backtest period ends before the exit time is reached, the position is closed at the last available bar. This prevents phantom open positions in backtest results.

Recommended Improvements

Future enhancements that would make this strategy more robust for live trading.

Stop Loss (1 ATR)

Add a trailing or fixed stop loss based on Average True Range to limit downside on individual trades.

Take Profit (2 ATR)

Lock in gains when the trade reaches 2x the ATR from entry. This creates a 1:2 risk-reward ratio.

Position Sizing

Risk a fixed percentage of equity per trade (e.g., 1%) instead of fixed 1-share lots.

Max Trades Per Day

Limit entries to prevent overtrading on choppy days. Typical cap: 3–5 trades per session.

Volume Filter

Require above-average volume on the entry bar to confirm institutional participation.

Walk-Forward Validation

Optimize parameters on in-sample data, then validate on out-of-sample. Prevents overfitting.

Execution Pipeline

1. Load Bars Fetch historical aggregates from PostgreSQL (pre-loaded via Market Data page).
2. Compute Indicators RSI, SMA (fast + slow), Stochastic %K/%D calculated over the full bar set.
3. Scan for Entries Walk forward through bars. When all 4 conditions are true and no position is open, mark entry at next bar.
4. Apply Exit Check each bar's timestamp. If at or past exit time, close the position and record the trade.
5. Compute Metrics Calculate total P&L, win rate, Sharpe ratio, max drawdown from the trade list.
Data Prerequisite Historical bars must already be in the database for your chosen ticker + date range. Use the Market Data or Stock Analysis page to fetch data first. The backtest engine reads from the local PostgreSQL store, not from Polygon.io directly.
