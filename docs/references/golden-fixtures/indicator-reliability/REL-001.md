# REL-001 — IC Hit Rate (Directional Consistency)

**Status:** active · v1 (2026-05-08)

**Canonical:** `PythonDataService/app/research/validation/ic.py::compute_information_coefficient`

## What this pins

`hit_rate` from `compute_information_coefficient`, validated against direct sign-consistency counting on 5 synthetic trading days.

## Oracle

hand_computed — hit_rate computed without calling `compute_information_coefficient`:

```text
daily_IC[d] = spearmanr(feature[day d], return[day d]).statistic
hit_rate    = count(daily_IC * sign(mean_IC) > 0) / N_days
```

A day "hits" when its IC shares the sign of the mean IC. Hit rate > 0.5 indicates a signal with persistent directional edge.

## Reference

Grinold & Kahn (1999) "Active Portfolio Management", Ch. 6. Hit rate as directional consistency metric.

## Tolerance

`atol=1e-9, rtol=0.0` — hit_rate is a rational number (count / N_days); float64 division.
